Three different machine learning models were built to uncover the specific relationship between the current Vix value and the S&P 500 return one year later.

Model 1:
A looping Neural Network

A screenshot of Model 1's "triple loop" which cycled through every unique pair of 21 securities, and then tested each of them against the future results of two other securities (the S&P 500 and oil futures). Each pass consisted of 420 tests, after which the other parameters (features used as model inputs, number of days used to determine the future return, etc.) were adjusted, and then the process was repeated.

In this chart, the future returns of S&P 500 are aligned with the current value of the Vix, showing the strong positive coorelation between positive S&P 500 returns and high Vix values. For potential investors, a good time to consider investing for a one-year period in the S&P 500 would be when the Vix is above 50, but a Vix over 35 has also yielded frequent positive results.
